Become a Member/Join Our Network

We are working to build a large-scale constituency of 2,000,000 peace advocates by 2030 around the world. We work with individuals, institutions and groups working for peace and development by taking actions to "Be the Peace" we are advocating for. To better serve the field, we also work with special focus to help strengthen and grow coalitions and alliances among like-minded groups and organizations.

Membership Description

Membership in this organization is also open to all those who care about, have an interest in, or wish to make contributions to help Global Youth Network for Peace foster a culture of peace among, between and within nations of the world.

Individual Membership at Global Youth Network for Peace is attained when a single individual secure a membership form at our membership directorate at the cost of $ 5 United States Dollars, complete and submit to the directorate. Our membership directorate does all validations and approve with an official letter confirming your membership status with us. Your data is then entered into our membership database.

    At a membership orientation/inaugural ceremony, all persons that hold Individual Membership Category with us are presented with the following benefits:

  1. GYNP Organizational Profile Handbook.
  2. GYNP Official Membership T. Shirt.
  3. GYNP Official Membership Identification Card.
  4. GYNP Official Universal Serial Identification Code.
  5. Gain recognition as a member of an international network of leaders, experts, professionals, practitioners, students and organizations who are dedicated to fostering a culture of peace around the world, and whose united voice is heard within the international community.
  6. Opportunity for enrollment into the GYNP School of Technology programs at a reduced cost.
  7. Networking opportunities.
  8. Automatic admission to the GYNP conferences, seminars, workshops, panel discussions, lectures, roundtables, and many other events, at a reduced fee.
  9. Satisfaction derived from giving back to the community through GYNP.

Offer Expert Service

Whether you are a university scholar, researcher, diplomat, practitioner, or policymaker; you have expertise in war, ethnic conflict, ethnic studies, sectarian conflict, racial conflict, race and ethnicity, tribal conflict, religious conflict, religious studies, theology, interfaith dialogue, pluralism, peace and conflict studies, conflict analysis and resolution, mediation, dialogue, conflict prevention, conflict management, conflict transformation, alternative dispute resolution, restorative justice, and peacebuilding, or related areas, disciplines and sectors; and you would like to offer your expert service, knowledge and skills to journalists, policymakers, governments, international institutions, regional, national and local organizations, individuals and groups. We invite you to secure and complete a form from our research and policy evaluation department and get listed on our expert portal as an expert in conflict resolution. This opportunity is also extended to a traditional ruler, leader of indigenous people, or faith leader, and your work in your community or congregation involves civil conflict resolution, mediation and dialogue, you are also invited to join our expert portal.

Expert Service Provider Categories

Career diplomats with expertise in ethnic conflict, tribal conflict, religious conflict, racial conflict, sectarian conflict, caste based conflict, culture based conflict, and conflict resolution.
Faith leaders whose roles in their communities or congregations include ethnic and religious conflict resolution, mediation, dialogue, and other forms of conflict resolution.
Independent or think tank affiliated researchers on ethnic conflict, tribal conflict, racial conflict, caste based conflict, religious conflict, sectarian conflict, culture based conflict, and conflict resolution.
Leaders of indigenous peoples whose roles in their communities include the resolution of ethnic conflict, tribal conflict, community conflict, land based dispute, inheritance conflict, culture based conflict, caste based conflict, racial conflict, religious conflict, sectarian conflict, and so on.
Public policy experts whose policy making interests focus on the resolution of ethnic conflict, tribal conflict, racial conflict, religious conflict, sectarian conflict, caste based conflict, and culture based conflict.
Practitioners in the field of ethnic conflict, tribal conflict, racial conflict, culture based conflict, caste based conflict, religious conflict, sectarian conflict, conflict resolution and peacebuilding.
Traditional rulers whose roles in their communities include the resolution of ethnic conflict, tribal conflict, community conflict, land based dispute, inheritance conflict, culture based conflict, caste based conflict, racial conflict, religious conflict, sectarian conflict, and so on.
University or academic experts in ethnic conflict, tribal conflict, racial conflict, culture based conflict, caste based conflict, religious conflict, sectarian conflict, and conflict resolution.

What to Expect?

After submitting the completed Expert Service Provider form, your profile will be reviewed, and the information you provided will be verified. If found accurate and eligible for inclusion in the Expert Service Provider portal, your expert profile will be approved the same day and added on our website. If additional information is needed to determine your eligibility, we will contact you. If we could not determine your qualification to be an Expert in Conflict Resolution, your application will be rejected.
