Our Journey to Sustainable Peacebuilding in Sierra Leone

This long journey all started on the 5th of March 2019 when a group of eight young, vibrant and passionate youths, headed by Mr. Thomas Kinsley Justice Lebbie held the very first meeting in Kambia after his meeting with the vissionair, Mr. Aloysius Tamba Foryoh in Freetown and exchanged ideas on starting a peace network under the auspice of the Youth for Peace and security Africa. This meeting laid the foundation to what is today known as Global youth Network for Peace.

The first meeting held to start what is today an empire of peacebuilders

Few days after the first meeting, the visionaire, Mr. Aloysius Tamba Foryoh advertised volunteer recruitment into the network and over one hundred applications were received. Then, the dream started becoming a reality, as about sixty three of the applicants were recruited, who in turned established the network in their local communities in fulfillment of their volunteer responsibilities.

This is a selection of pictorial evidence of few of the reports that filtered in our database by community volunteers acting as network coordinators across the country few months after its establishment.

In the Thunder Hill community, east end of Freetown, our work got largely influenced by Mr. Sulaiman Bull-Hassan who established the network in the community and mobilized the largest network of young people as of May, 2019.

Then, there came another hero from within, Agibu Jalloh established the network in the circular road community attracting large membership.

In Kailahun, Eastern Sierra Leone, Augustine dedicated much of the organization’s work with school going children as a way of inculcating in them the culture of peace.

Sheikh Ahmed Bangura, an uprising artist and a liberal in the Brima lane community believes in the power of music/entertainment in preaching peace to the younger generation. His daily interaction with kids and ghetto youths makes him famous and influential in mobilizing people to a common goal.

Mrs Jessica Sandy, joined our network few months after our establishment, as busy as she was; balancing between office work and daily choirs as a house wife, she was able to build one of the competitive networks in upper Wellington.

In Kambia, the team did not only hold credit for convening the first meeting, they embarked on making peace a reality tour to schools in and around Kambia town, thus mobilizing dozens of their peers to the common goal of sustainable peacebuilding.

In Kakuna and its surroundings, Mr. Mohamed Mansaray and Mr. Alimammy Rassin Wurie have conducted several community dialogues on local peacebuilding efforts.

The city of Bo, Southern Sierra Leone got the network established by Mr. Steven Koroma and large number of student at the paramedical college got interested and joined.

Sequentially, other networks were established in Anglo, West of Freetown by Mr. Thomas, Calaba town, eastern Freetown by Mr. Amadu Tarawally, Grafton, by Mr. Jonathan K. Sesay, Bottom Oku by Mr. Elogima Mcarthy etc.

Our journey to sustainable peacebuilding has been filled with glamour, seeing the productive results of our work in fruition. Using the bottom top approach, our work has yielded a lot of fruits on the lives of those we serve.

Here is the second phase of our journey. Moving few steps beyond the ladder...............

Haven gained the massive support of the local communities, on 5th May, 2019, we trained 63 of our community volunteers on conflict resolution, human rights advocacy and local leadership.

Another remarkable moment for us was the training and certification of 67 young Sierra Leoneans on website designing, blogging, digital marketing and social media branding to empower them for productivity through digital technology.

From holding two successful pre-conferences to convening the first ever National Youth Conference on Peace and Development in Sierra Leone, drawing a well over 120 youths from across the country featuring inspiring Sierra Leoneans with global experience in peacbuilding and youth development as keynote speakers, guest speakers and moderators.

Where are we now?

The National Youth Conference on Peace and Development wasn’t just a talking shop/show, it was a gathering of Sierra Leone’s brightest minds in the field of peacebuilding and youth development, a platform that gave young people the opportunity to hear and be heard on the way forward for Sierra Leone’s attainment of sustainable peace and development through individual and collective efforts, mostly, the youths. It laid a foundation for the development of the first ever youth resolution developed by the youths themselves.

In the resolution, young people call for the government and private sector to:-

  • Humanize justice systems.
  • Foster on equity, social justice, and the promotion of democratic principles and the rule of law in our society.
  • Allocation of special funds for local peacebuilding initiatives
  • Making peace a reality by inclusive participation and many others found in NYCPD Resolution 2019; the voice of young people, reflecting global challenges.

This prompted us to design a project tagged “project 1,000” meant to direct the talent, energy, time and efforts of about 200 young Sierra Leoneans in direct peacebuilding efforts in local communities in Freetown, Bo, Kenema and Makeni respectively to domesticate and conceptualize peacebuilding process in Sierra Leone.

It is a one year project with possibility of extension that would see the establishment of peace clubs in 120 schools in the four cities highlighted above; the establishment of community peace dialogue forums in 120 communities in Freetown, Bo, Kenema and Makeni respectively, training of 200 young Sierra Leoneans on website designing, digital marketing and mobile application development and hosting the second edition of the Youth Conference on Peace and Development.

You can request for these document for more details; NYCPD Resolution 2019; the voice of young people, reflecting global challenges, Project 1,000:domesticating and conceptualizing peacebuilding process in Sierra Leone and Global Youth Network for Peace 2020 Work Plan. These three documents are no doubt a roadmap to sustainable peace and development in Sierra Leone.

235 young Sierra Leoneans out of the 585 that applied to volunteer with us have successfully undergone interview with us and are waiting to start work with us.
